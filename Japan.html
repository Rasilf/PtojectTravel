日本の思い出
My Japanese Poetry !

From the first breath of crisp air in Kyoto to the dazzling energy of Tokyo’s nights, my time in Japan was a collection of moments that felt like poetry. I found magic in the small things: the polite bow of a stranger, the steam rising from a bowl of ramen, and the pink petals of Sakura falling like snow. This travel journal is a window into those days—a reflection of a culture that honors its past while fearlessly embracing the future.

Wisdom for the Journey

  1. Get a JR Pass: It is the cheapest and fastest way to travel between cities via the Bullet Train (Shinkansen).

  2. Keep Cash Handy: Although modern, many small restaurants and traditional shrines only accept cash.

  3. Rent a Pocket Wi-Fi: It is essential for navigating the complex train systems and translation.

  4. Master the Etiquette: A simple bow and keeping your phone silent on trains shows great respect for the local culture.

  5. Tech Essentials: Use Google Maps for precise train platforms and Google Lens to translate menus in real-time.

  6. The Konbini Magic: Don't miss the high-quality meals at 7-Eleven or Lawson; they are delicious, fresh, and very affordable!
